Lake Chala

Imagine a Lake within two nations. Lake Chala comprises of a shore in Tanzania and Kenya. It has a bewitchingly beautiful landscape with its remarkable blue color. It exists below unsuspecting plains. Friendly fishermen exists to provide lake monster stories as part of local folklore. During good moments, one might spot monitor lizards, baboons, and vervet monkeys as well as a dik-dik or bushbuck. In Chala's cliff-faces, the bird species are more than 250 species including peregrine falcons.
